Review

RxSwift by the Ray Wenderlich team stands out as the best RxSwift related book in the stores. It starts out with a soft introduction to its syntax and goals, which is quite important since FRP mindset is quite different from the usual imperative style programming. It follows up with chapters dedicated to RxSwift's operators and each chapter ends up with exercises that I advise every person to do, because RxSwift might sound easy at the start but there's much to learn and without practicing and "brainstorming" you'll probabaly end up lost. Near the end of book, it attempts to put most of operators from the previous chapters together and gives you real world problems and examples. As previously said, I really recommend this book to anyone interested in learning RxSwift, it's just so good that sometimes I end up going back to it to read about a specific operator that I want to use for the first time.
